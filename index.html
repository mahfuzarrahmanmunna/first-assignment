Bangladesh 2.0 aims to redefine the nation’s trajectory by embracing cutting-edge technology, promoting inclusive growth, and ensuring sustainability. Modern Infrastructure

Building state-of-the-art infrastructure to support economic growth and global competitiveness.

Dr. Muhammad Yunus is a Bangladeshi economist and Nobel Peace Prize laureate known as the "father of microfinance." He founded the Grameen Bank in 1983, providing small, collateral-free loans to empower the poor, especially women. Yunus introduced the concept of "social business," focusing on solving societal problems rather than maximizing profits. In 2006, he and Grameen Bank jointly received the Nobel Peace Prize for their efforts to combat poverty. His work has transformed global perspectives on financial inclusion and inspired movements in social entrepreneurship.
